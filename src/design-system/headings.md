It's important to point out that the size of the heading does not convey its importance within the context of a page. An extra small heading has as much chance of being an `<h2>` as a large heading does, the size differences are primarily down to the aesthetics of the page.

Extra large (XL) headings are for page titles. They get to be special because they're a different colour and all stretched out.

Large, medium, small and extra small (XS) headings are generally used for defining the content hierarchy, in whatever way that works best.

Extra extra small (or 2XS) headings are used for headings that headline a section of a page, but generally don't have good reason to stand out.

## Subheadings

Subheadings are used for pre-title and post-title additions to headings. They never appear by themselves.

{{ kimExample("typography-subheadings", "Subheadings") }}

Subheadings only exist for XL, large and medium headings.
